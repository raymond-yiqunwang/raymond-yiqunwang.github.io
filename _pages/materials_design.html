Materials Science & Engineering


Figure 3 | Data-driven electronic materials design and discovery roadmap. By leveraging first principles simulations, statistical learning models, and adaptive optimization algorithms, we achieve a more cost-effective design framework for novel electronic materials.

During my Ph.D. studies, I developed a data-driven materials design framework by threading multiple research subdomains, i.e., first principles density functional theory (DFT) simulations, materials informatics, and optimization theory. Electronic materials research is intimately connected to condensed matter physics and quantum chemistry, with more focus on inorganic crystals with periodic boundary conditions. The materials systems under investigation have high degrees of freedom in terms of chemical compositions, microscopic lattice structures, as well as electronic/magnetic interactions. I start to realize that we may never have a universal solver to achieve "one model to solve them all". Therefore, interdisciplinary research is worth more attention owing to its power in bringing scientists and engineers with various backgrounds together to solve existing challenges. I enjoy learning new concepts and research methodologies from other domain experts. For more details on my work in this realm, please visit the Materials Science page.

Adaptive Optimization for Materials Design

I developed an adaptive optimization engine for electronic materials design.


Figure 2 | Upper panel: The workflow of a conventional feature-based machine learning model. Lower panel: The adaptive materials discovery scheme.

The optimization scheme starts from an initial set of design of experiments, where system variables, design objectives, and design space are first defined for the problem. Property evaluation: The target material properties (design objectives) are evaluated either by experimental measurement or theoretical simulations. Candidate composition and its evaluated properties are then added to a Data repository, which initially may either be empty or only contains entries for existing materials within the design space. Its size grows as more candidate materials are evaluated during the adaptive optimization process.


Animation | The adaptive optimization history, where grey diamonds are the initial DOE set, blue circles are newly explored compositions, and orange stars form the design Pareto front.

Featureless learning involves directly learning from the chemical composition of materials comprising the data repository by mapping each compositional variable into a two-dimensional latent space using maximum likelihood estimation, which enables the construction of a latent variable Gaussian process (LVGP) surrogate model. knowledge within the data repository. Composition optimization: Multi-objective Bayesian optimization is then performed with the LVGP models to obtain the next candidate material composition with the highest expected maximin improvement. The iterative optimization continues until all compounds satisfying the objectives are discovered, forming the Pareto front, or research resources are exhausted.

Materials Informatics

Keeping the numerical optimization methods and their limitations in mind, I realized the significance of statistical learning for us to further understand materials physics. To that end, I made some statistical analysis on crystal structure databases using a deep neural network. We used structure factors in the form of 3D point cloud to uniquely represent crystal structures and chemical compositions, and analyzed the correlation between symmetry, electron density, and materials properties. This work is still under review at Physical Review Research.


Figure 3 | Learning the Fourier space crystal structure genome for property classificaitons.


Lastly, I explore the potential of using symbolic learning to reveal some governing physical laws from a large amount of data. Symbolic regression has recently garnered much attention from the scientific research community owing to its form-free learning mechanism, which makes it an ideal tool for materials scientists to analyze their experimental data.
