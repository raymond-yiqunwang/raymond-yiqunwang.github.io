Decoding Intermolecular Interactions from a Data-driven Perspective

In our lab, researchers with various education background (e.g., AI, chemistry, biology) work collaboratively on the most challenging biological problems. Our goal is to develop novel AI models to understand biomolecular systems and reveal the governing physical laws from a data-driven perspective.

Specifically, I am interested in teaching AI models to think like a theoretical chemist (Figure 1). Instead of purely making inference from data, I try to enforce the model to learn physical rules (e.g., symmetry constraints) by hard-coding them into the model architecture. I apologize for not being able to introduce too many details here at this stage. However, I would be happy to have individual discussions to share some ideas and explore potential collaborations. In addition, our team will share our research advances with the community once we make significant breakthroughs. Cheers.
