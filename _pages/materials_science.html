Adaptive Optimization for Materials Design

I developed an adaptive optimization engine for electronic materials design.


Figure 2. Upper panel: The workflow of a conventional feature-based machine learning model. Lower panel: The adaptive materials discovery scheme.

The optimization scheme starts from an initial set of design of experiments, where system variables, design objectives, and design space are first defined for the problem. Property evaluation: The target material properties (design objectives) are evaluated either by experimental measurement or theoretical simulations. Candidate composition and its evaluated properties are then added to a Data repository, which initially may either be empty or only contains entries for existing materials within the design space. Its size grows as more candidate materials are evaluated during the adaptive optimization process.


Animation: The adaptive optimization history.

Featureless learning involves directly learning from the chemical composition of materials comprising the data repository by mapping each compositional variable into a two-dimensional latent space (spanned by z1 and z2) using maximum likelihood estimation, which enables the construction of a latent variable Gaussian process (LVGP) surrogate model. knowledge within the data repository. Composition optimization: Multi-objective Bayesian optimization is then performed with the LVGP models to obtain the next candidate material composition with the highest expected maximin improvement. The iterative optimization continues until all compounds satisfying the objectives are discovered, forming the Pareto front, or research resources expire.

Materials Informatics

Keeping the numerical optimization methods and their limitations in mind, I realized the significance of statistical learning for us to further understand materials physics. To that end, I made some statistical analysis on crystal structure databases using a deep neural network. We used structure factors in the form of 3D point cloud to uniquely represent crystal structures and chemical compositions, and analyzed the correlation between symmetry, electron density, and materials properties.
